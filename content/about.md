## $ whoami

I'm an engineer based in the EU, about two years into a Cloud / DevOps career.

By day I work with Azure, Terraform, Ansible, Kubernetes, and CI/CD pipelines. In my free time I run a homelab — partly for services my family actually uses, partly to work through whatever I'm currently learning. Not only to keep up with the industry, but because I enjoy it.

This blog is where those two contexts meet.

## What I write about

- **Homelab experiments** — what I build on real hardware at home, what breaks, and what I learn from fixing it
- **Production lessons** — sanitized stories from work, with what I would have done differently
- **Architecture decisions** — opinions on tools and tradeoffs, with the reasoning behind them
- **Career reflections** — occasionally, when the perspective from this stage of the path seems useful

## Why I write here

Two reasons.

The first is selfish: writing crystallizes learning. If I can't explain a concept clearly, I don't actually understand it.

The second is for engineers earlier on the path than I am. There's plenty of expert content from senior engineers explaining architectures from the top of the mountain. There's plenty of beginner tutorial content. There's much less in between — engineers a few steps ahead, sharing the path while it's still fresh, including the wrong turns. That's the gap I'm trying to write into.

## What this blog isn't

Not a tutorial site. If you're looking for "how to install X step by step," there are better places. The posts here lean toward decisions, tradeoffs, and reflection — closer to engineering writing than documentation.

Not a portfolio in the CV sense. The CV lives elsewhere; this is the work itself, ongoing.
